My Engineering Design Process#

This process is heavily inspired by the Framing, Diverging, Converging and Representing (FDCR) model taught in Praxis at UofT. It also borrows from (Design Council, 2006) who begin their process with a Discovery phase and with a Deliver phase. Likewise, my process includes an Identifying Opportunities section to the FDCR process at the beginning and I close the loop by conducting Stakeholder Validation. The tools, models and frameworks invoked along the way arise from a range of sources and are more thoroughly explained and analyzed throughout this handbook.

Working On A Team#

Working with others on design problems has been a fascinating experience. All team members I have worked with have brought unique perspectives to the table. These are a few notes about working on teams that are helpful to note:

  • Break the ice. You will likely be working on a project for a while and so it is best to get to know your team members.
  • Establish team values. This is important as it will impact the way DfXs are prioritized by the team.
  • Establish team norms (expectations such as punctuality, accountability, and so on and so forth).
  • Listen to others well.
  • Contribute but don't anchor discussions.
  • Be aware of personal biases.
